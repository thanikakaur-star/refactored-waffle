Healthcare Tender Alerts: How to Never Miss an Opportunity

Healthcare tender alerts notify you when a new procurement opportunity matching your criteria is published. The challenge is that each government portal has its own separate alert system, so to cover the whole market you either juggle several inboxes of notifications or use one tool that aggregates them all. Here's how tender alerts work and how to set them up so nothing slips through.

Why tender alerts matter

Procurement deadlines are often tight — sometimes just a few weeks from publication to submission. If you only discover a tender late, you may not have time to prepare a competitive bid. Alerts flip the model: instead of you checking portals every day, the opportunities come to you the moment they're posted, giving you maximum time to respond.

How alerts work on each portal

SourceAlert method
TED EuropaSaved searches with email notifications, filtered by CPV code and country
SAM.govSaved search alerts by NAICS/PSC code and keyword
NHS / Find a TenderSaved searches with email alerts by keyword and category
WHO & partnersNotices published across WHO and partner channels (less centralised)

Each works reasonably well on its own — but if you sell across regions, you end up managing four or five separate alert systems, each with different filters and formats.

How to set up effective alerts

  • Use precise filters — classification codes (CPV and NAICS) cut noise far better than keywords alone
  • Set value thresholds — filter out tenders too small or too large for you
  • Filter by region — only the markets you can actually serve
  • Review and refine — tune your filters over a few weeks to balance coverage against noise

The problem with portal-by-portal alerts

The native alerts are free, but fragmented. You can't see all your opportunities in one view, you can't compare across markets, and the formats and currencies differ. It's easy to miss something simply because it landed in the wrong inbox. For suppliers selling internationally, this fragmentation is a real risk — exactly the problem covered in manual tracking vs a platform.

Frequently asked questions

Are tender alerts free?

The government portals offer free alerts for their own data. Aggregated, cross-portal alerts in one place are what paid tools provide.

How quickly will I be alerted to a new tender?

It depends on the source and tool, but the goal is to surface new opportunities within a day of publication so you have maximum time to bid.

Can I filter alerts to just my product category?

Yes — using classification codes and category filters, you can narrow alerts to exactly the kind of contracts you bid on.
