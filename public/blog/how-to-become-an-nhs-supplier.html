How to Become an NHS Supplier: A Step-by-Step Guide

To become an NHS supplier you need to register on the right procurement portals, meet the NHS's quality and compliance standards, get onto a relevant framework or win a tender, and then deliver reliably. The NHS spends over £15 billion a year through procurement — but it's a structured, standards-driven buyer, so getting in takes preparation. Here's the step-by-step path.

Step 1: Understand how the NHS buys

The NHS doesn't buy as one organisation. Purchasing happens through several routes: NHS Supply Chain (the central buying organisation), individual NHS trusts, regional procurement hubs, and cross-government bodies like Crown Commercial Service. Most large, recurring purchases run through framework agreements. Knowing which route fits your product is the first step.

Step 2: Register on the key portals

You'll need to be visible where NHS opportunities are published:

  • Find a Tender Service (FTS) — high-value, above-threshold contracts
  • Contracts Finder — public contracts above £12,000
  • NHS Supply Chain — to get onto national frameworks
  • Individual trust e-tendering portals (Atamis, In-Tend, etc.) for local tenders

For the full map of where NHS opportunities live, see how to find NHS tenders.

Step 3: Get your compliance in order

The NHS holds suppliers to clear standards. Before you bid, make sure you have:

  • Relevant quality certifications (e.g. ISO 13485 for medical devices, UKCA/CE marking)
  • Appropriate insurance and financial standing
  • Compliance with NHS terms and conditions and data/security requirements
  • Modern slavery, sustainability and social value policies (increasingly weighted in evaluations)

Step 4: Get onto a framework (or win a tender)

For most suppliers, the goal is getting onto an NHS framework — a pre-approved list buyers can purchase from. When a framework in your category is tendered or renewed, you bid to be included. Miss the window and you may wait years, so tracking renewal dates matters. Alternatively, you can bid directly on individual tenders as they're published. Either way, a compliant, well-evidenced bid that addresses the published criteria is what wins.

Step 5: Find the opportunities early

The single biggest practical challenge is simply finding relevant NHS opportunities in time to prepare a strong bid — they're spread across FTS, Contracts Finder, NHS Supply Chain, and dozens of trust portals. Checking them all daily is a real burden, and missing a tight deadline means missing the contract entirely.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to be UK-based to supply the NHS?

No, but you'll need to meet the technical, quality and compliance requirements of each tender, and typically have a way to deliver and support within the UK.

How long does it take to become an NHS supplier?

It varies by route. Getting compliance and registration in place can take weeks; winning a place on a framework depends on when relevant frameworks are tendered.

Is it free to register and bid?

The official portals are free to use. The cost is the time to monitor them and prepare compliant bids.
