Guru Gobind Singh Ji & the Panj Piare

ILLUSTRATION PLACEHOLDER

In 1699 at Anandpur Sahib, Guru Gobind Singh Ji asked who would give their life for their faith. Five brave Sikhs stepped forward — they became the Panj Piare (Five Beloved Ones). Guru Ji created the Khalsa, a community of saint-soldiers committed to justice and equality.

Vaisakhi — The Harvest Festival

ILLUSTRATION PLACEHOLDER

Clean black-and-white coloring book page, joyful celebration scene outline of Vaisakhi festival, people performing Bhangra and Giddha dances in traditional Panjabi clothing (kurta pajama, phulkari dupatta, ghagra), dhol drummer in centre, Nishan Sahib flag and bunting decorations, wheat harvest sheaves in foreground, decorative border of wheat ears and marigold flowers, thick bold outlines no shading no fill, pure line art on white background, 8.5x11 portrait layout, professional coloring book quality

Vaisakhi is celebrated on April 13th or 14th each year. It marks the Panjabi harvest festival and, for Sikhs, the birth of the Khalsa in 1699. Communities celebrate with Nagar Kirtan (street processions), Bhangra dancing, and sharing festive foods.

ਵਿਸਾਖੀ (Vaisakhi) — Harvest festival / Khalsa birthday • ਭੰਗੜਾ (Bhangra) — Traditional Panjabi dance

A Gurdwara ('Gateway to the Guru') is the Sikh place of worship. It has four doors to show it is open to people of every direction and background. Inside, the Guru Granth Sahib Ji is read, Kirtan (hymns) are sung, and everyone shares Langar together.

Phulkari — Flower Embroidery

ILLUSTRATION PLACEHOLDER

Clean black-and-white coloring book page, full-page geometric Phulkari embroidery pattern design featuring traditional motifs: repeated diamond shapes filled with star patterns, peacocks, wheat stalks, and stylized flowers arranged in a symmetrical grid, border of traditional Panjabi textile trim patterns, designed to look like a fabric swatch ready to be colored in vibrant colors, thick bold outlines no shading no fill, pure line art on white background, 8.5x11 portrait layout, professional coloring book quality

Phulkari means 'flower work.' It is the traditional embroidery art of Panjab, created by women using bright silk threads on handspun cotton cloth. Each region of Panjab has its own patterns. A Bagh ('garden') is a Phulkari so densely stitched that no base fabric shows through.

ਫੁਲਕਾਰੀ (Phulkari) — Flower embroidery • ਬਾਗ (Bagh) — Garden / fully embroidered cloth

Panjabi Alphabet — Gurmukhi

ILLUSTRATION PLACEHOLDER

Clean black-and-white coloring book page, the full Gurmukhi alphabet arranged in a beautiful grid layout, each of the 35 letters drawn in large ornamental outlined font style with decorative flourishes, small illustration icons next to key letters showing objects that start with that sound (like a children's alphabet poster), decorative border of open books and ink pots with quill pens, thick bold outlines no shading no fill, pure line art on white background, 8.5x11 portrait layout, professional coloring book quality

Gurmukhi means 'from the mouth of the Guru.' This script was standardised by Guru Angad Dev Ji so that everyone could read and write, regardless of caste. The Guru Granth Sahib Ji is written in Gurmukhi. The alphabet has 35 letters and is written left to right.

ਗੁਰਮੁਖੀ (Gurmukhi) — From the Guru's mouth / Panjabi script • ਊੜਾ ਐੜਾ (Oora Aira) — First letters of Gurmukhi alphabet

Mai Bhago — The Warrior Saint

ILLUSTRATION PLACEHOLDER

Clean black-and-white coloring book page, powerful portrait outline of Mai Bhago riding a horse in full warrior armour, holding a spear aloft, flowing dupatta and turban trailing in the wind, horse in mid-gallop with detailed mane and tail, dust clouds at hooves, background showing the battlefield of Muktsar with distant fortifications, decorative border of shields and spears with rose and lotus flower accents, thick bold outlines no shading no fill, pure line art on white background, 8.5x11 portrait layout, professional coloring book quality

Mai Bhago was a courageous Sikh warrior who led 40 soldiers back into battle at Muktsar in 1705. She fought alongside Guru Gobind Singh Ji and survived the battle. She is remembered as a symbol of bravery and a reminder that Sikh women have always been warriors and leaders.

ਮਾਈ ਭਾਗੋ (Mai Bhago) — Legendary Sikh woman warrior • ਮੁਕਤਸਰ (Muktsar) — Pool of liberation (battle site)
