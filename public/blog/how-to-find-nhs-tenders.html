How to Find NHS Tenders in 2026: A Complete Guide

The fastest way to find NHS tenders is to monitor three sources: the NHS Supply Chain framework portal, the UK government's Find a Tender Service (FTS), and Contracts Finder. The NHS spends over £15 billion a year through procurement, and opportunities are spread across these portals plus individual NHS trust websites. This guide explains exactly where to look, how NHS frameworks work, and how to make sure you never miss a relevant contract.

Where are NHS tenders published?

Unlike a single marketplace, NHS procurement opportunities are scattered across several official portals. Knowing which one to check depends on the size and type of the contract:

PortalWhat it covers
Find a Tender Service (FTS)High-value contracts above the procurement threshold (the post-Brexit replacement for OJEU/TED in the UK)
Contracts FinderUK public sector contracts above £12,000, including lower-value NHS opportunities
NHS Supply ChainNational framework agreements managed centrally on behalf of all NHS trusts
Individual NHS trust portalsLocal tenders run directly by hospitals and trusts (often on e-tendering platforms like Atamis or In-Tend)

Understanding NHS framework agreements

Most large NHS purchases happen through framework agreements rather than one-off tenders. A framework is a pre-approved list of suppliers that NHS bodies can buy from without running a full procurement each time. If you want to sell to the NHS at scale, getting onto the right framework is usually the goal.

The main NHS framework providers are:

  • NHS Supply Chain — the central buying organisation for the NHS in England, covering everything from surgical instruments to capital equipment
  • NHS Commercial Solutions, NOE CPC, and other regional collaborative procurement hubs
  • Crown Commercial Service (CCS) — cross-government frameworks the NHS also uses, especially for IT and corporate services
  • HealthTrust Europe and other private framework operators

How to search NHS tenders effectively

1. Set up alerts on every portal

Both Find a Tender and Contracts Finder let you save searches and receive email alerts. Set these up with keywords relevant to your products (for example "infusion pumps," "wound care," or "diagnostic reagents"). The problem is that each portal has its own alert system, and none of them talk to each other.

2. Use CPV codes to filter precisely

NHS tenders are classified using CPV codes (Common Procurement Vocabulary). Filtering by the CPV codes that match your category dramatically reduces noise. For example, 33100000 covers medical equipment, while 33600000 covers pharmaceutical products.

3. Watch framework renewal dates

Frameworks run for fixed periods (usually 2–4 years). If you miss the window to bid for a framework, you may wait years for the next one. Tracking renewal dates is one of the highest-value things a supplier can do.

The problem: NHS tenders are fragmented

Here's the challenge every healthcare supplier faces. NHS procurement isn't in one place — it's spread across FTS, Contracts Finder, NHS Supply Chain, and dozens of individual trust portals. Checking all of them every day is a full-time job, and it's easy to miss a relevant opportunity buried in the wrong portal.

It gets harder if you sell internationally. A medical device company supplying both the NHS and EU hospitals also has to monitor TED Europa, and if they sell to the US, SAM.gov as well. That's four or five separate systems, each with different search interfaces and alert mechanisms.

Frequently asked questions

Are NHS tenders free to access?

Yes. The official portals (Find a Tender, Contracts Finder) are free to search. The cost is the time it takes to monitor them all consistently, which is where aggregation tools save you hours each week.

Do I need to be UK-based to bid on NHS tenders?

No. International suppliers can bid on NHS contracts, though you'll need to meet the technical, quality, and compliance requirements set out in each tender, and typically have a way to deliver and support within the UK.

How often are new NHS tenders published?

New opportunities appear daily across the various portals. High-value framework opportunities are less frequent but far more significant — which is why tracking renewal cycles matters.
