How to Win Government Healthcare Contracts: A Beginner's Guide

To win government healthcare contracts, you need to find the right tenders early, understand exactly what the buyer is asking for, submit a compliant and competitive bid, and price it based on what similar contracts actually go for. Governments buy hundreds of billions in healthcare goods and services every year — but most suppliers lose simply because they find opportunities too late or bid blind on price. This guide walks through the fundamentals.

Step 1: Find the right opportunities (early)

Government healthcare contracts are advertised on public procurement portals. The major ones are TED Europa (EU), SAM.gov (US federal), NHS Supply Chain and Find a Tender (UK), and WHO (global health). The earlier you find a relevant tender, the more time you have to prepare a strong bid — and tight deadlines are the number-one reason suppliers miss out.

Step 2: Read the tender documents carefully

Every tender comes with documents setting out the requirements, evaluation criteria, and submission rules. Read them in full before deciding to bid. Pay close attention to:

  • Eligibility criteria — certifications, quality standards, minimum turnover
  • Technical requirements — exactly what's being bought and to what spec
  • Evaluation weighting — how much is price vs quality vs other factors
  • Deadlines and format — miss these and you're disqualified, however good your bid

Step 3: Decide whether to bid (bid/no-bid)

You can't win every contract, and bidding well takes real effort. Be honest about fit: can you genuinely meet the requirements, deliver on time, and compete on price? A disciplined bid/no-bid decision means you put your energy into the tenders you can actually win.

Step 4: Write a compliant, compelling bid

Compliance comes first — answer every question, in the format requested, with all required documents. Then make it compelling: show you understand the buyer's needs, evidence your track record, and be specific rather than generic. Public buyers score against published criteria, so address each one explicitly.

Step 5: Price it using real market data

This is where many suppliers go wrong — they guess. Price too high and you lose; too low and you win unprofitable work. The smart approach is to benchmark against what similar contracts have actually been awarded for. Contract award data shows you who won comparable tenders and at what value, so you can pitch your price in the right range. This is exactly the kind of intelligence that separates consistent winners from the rest.

Common reasons suppliers lose

  • Finding the tender too late to prepare properly
  • Missing a mandatory requirement or document (instant disqualification)
  • Pricing blind, with no sense of the market rate
  • Generic bids that don't address the published criteria
  • Not tracking framework renewal dates and missing the window entirely

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to be a big company to win government contracts?

No. Many contracts are open to small and mid-size suppliers, and some markets (like US federal) reserve contracts specifically for small businesses through set-asides.

How long does the bidding process take?

It varies — from a few weeks for smaller tenders to several months for large frameworks. Finding opportunities early gives you the time you need.

Is it free to find and bid on these contracts?

The official portals are free to search. The cost is the time to monitor them consistently, which is where aggregation tools save you hours.
